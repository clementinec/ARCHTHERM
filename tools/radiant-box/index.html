What This Tool Does

This is a teaching model, not a certification tool. It assumes a rectangular room, uniform surface temperatures, a point-like body location, and a well-mixed air temperature. The view factors are estimated by casting rays from the body point and counting which surface each ray reaches first.

For A2, use it to make a bounded claim: Position B differs from Position A because the body sees more of a warmer or cooler surface. Export the CSV and PNG, then add your own plan/section and uncertainty note.

Grasshopper Translation

  1. Define room or urban-canyon surfaces and assign surface temperatures.
  2. Define one or more body points at seated or standing height.
  3. Estimate view factors by ray intersection or a Ladybug view-analysis route.
  4. Compute sum(F_i * T_i^4) in Kelvin and convert back to deg C.
  5. Compute operative temperature using a declared weighting.
  6. Export a table that shows inputs, view factors, T_mrt, T_op, and limits.

Use this tool as a transparent proxy for A2. If the real project needs solar position, complex geometry, outdoor longwave sky, or body orientation, state those as limits or move to Ladybug/Honeybee/GH.
