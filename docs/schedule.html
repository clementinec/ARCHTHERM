Schedule

The semester is structured around four accumulative artifacts. Weeks 1-6 teach physical and sensing concepts; Weeks 7-12 turn those concepts into stress tests, design action, validation, and defense.

Week Main Question Concepts In-Class Artifact Assignment
1 Why is air temperature not enough? Ta, RH, air speed, stagnant air, ventilation context, psychrometrics, point-to-field mapping, moisture action ladder preview, hidden defaults thermal claim autopsy + field-map teaser A1 launch
2 How does a body exchange heat with space? metabolism, clothing, convection, draft/breeze, radiation, evaporation, dehumidification, heat-stress pathways, PMV/PPD, adaptive comfort, UTCI, WBGT, heat index body heat-balance sketch A1 evidence setup
3 What can sensing prove? sensors, sampling bias, spatial field, interpolation, RH/Ta capture limits, air-movement observation, ventilation versus local exposure Ta/RH/air-movement transect + point/contour protocol A1 due
4 Why does radiant environment change the reading? surface temperature, MRT, operative temperature, outdoor exposure radiant plan/section A2 launch
5 How does radiant asymmetry become architecture? view factor, orientation, glass, shade, flux delivery radiant exchange card A2 due
6 When does heat build up? capacity, lag, heat balance, degree-hours, thresholds, condensation, mold risk, material moisture storage temporal storyboard + pulse check A3 launch
7 What is a simulation output actually? EnergyPlus exposure states, Ta/MRT/Top/RH, airflow-network and CFD solver literacy hourly state log A3 development
8 What changes when weather changes? EPW, TMY, GCM/RCM, stress windows, future weather weather stress memo A3 development
9 What does it take to reach a target? inverse query, found/censored action ladder action ladder checkpoint A3 due / A4 launch
10 What must change architecturally? failure modes, thresholds, pivot logic, suppress/buffer/redirect/celebrate heterogeneity design action alternatives A4 development
11 Does the action survive changed assumptions? roll-forward validation, uncertainty, reproducibility validation note + defense clinic A4 draft
12 Can the thermal argument be defended? synthesis, limits, design consequence thermal design jury A4 due

Rhythm

Each week has a short conceptual lecture, a worked example, and an artifact-building session. Session 2 is a diagnostic round: students bring one design image, classmates make educated guesses under the weekly lens, and the host reveals the actual thermal question, mitigation, and evidence limit.

Session 2 Diagnostic Round

Phase Time What Happens Output
Candidate scan 10 min students choose their own or existing design image one plan, section, rendering, or site photo
Slack post 5 min host posts image and minimal context; diagnosis stays hidden shared image thread
Round-table guesses 20-25 min classmates guess problem spot, mechanism, intervention, and competing constraint annotated guesses / votes
Host reveal + translation 10-15 min host explains actual issue, mitigation, evidence, and uncertainty assignment seed or revision note

Hints are generous in Weeks 1-3, moderate in Weeks 4-8, and deliberately thinner in Weeks 9-12.

Week 6 Pulse Check

An anonymous 5-minute pulse check asks:

  • Is the course matching what you expected?
  • Which concept or tool route needs more support?
  • What is unclear about A2, A3, or the final package?
  • What should be adjusted before the second half of semester?

The instructor summarizes one or two adjustments in Week 7.
