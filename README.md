# ARCH7XXX Course Planning Skeleton

Working folder for a new MArch-facing course on thermal environments, uncertainty, and architectural consequence.

This is not a redesign of ARCH7476. It reuses only the course-build discipline learned from ARCH7476:

- state the MArch-facing promise early
- anchor technical content in architectural situations
- make weekly outputs visible
- provide bounded, inspectable tools
- require uncertainty and reproducibility
- add mid-semester pulse feedback
- review the course through skeptical MArch and program director lenses

## Instructor Context

Instructor: Hongshan Guo, Assistant Professor, Department of Architecture, The University of Hong Kong.

The course should make the instructor's research profile visible without turning into a private research seminar. The relevant public research spine from FORGE is human-building-climate systems: thermal comfort and human heat, mean radiant temperature, climate futures and stochastic weather, EnergyPlus/co-simulation, risk-aware control, uncertainty, and design-facing pedagogy.

Reference site: <https://thermalcomfort.zone/>

## Working Title Options

1. Thermal Fields in Architecture: Bodies, Moisture, Radiation, and Climate Stress
2. Thermal Environments Beyond the Average
3. Thermal Environments: Radiation, Heterogeneity, and Human Experience
4. Thermal Fields, Failure, and Design Action

## Current Course Proposition

Thermal design is not the delivery of one average indoor condition. It is the shaping of heterogeneous thermal fields across bodies, moisture, radiation, surfaces, rooms, buildings, and climate stress under uncertainty.

Students learn to translate thermal evidence into architectural decisions by mapping variation, stress-testing assumptions, identifying failure modes, and proposing interventions that can be defended spatially, numerically, and humanly.

The default semester assumption is now **12 teaching weeks**, with room for holidays, review compression, and final submission timing.
