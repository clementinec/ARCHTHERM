# Review Prompt: Program Director

Read the course as a program director evaluating whether the course is coherent, defensible, and appropriately positioned as a MArch elective.

## Review Stance

You are looking for academic credibility, architectural relevance, assessment clarity, workload realism, and alignment with postgraduate expectations.

## Questions

1. Does the course title and description clearly signal a MArch-level architecture elective?
2. Does the course avoid sounding like HVAC training or a narrow software course?
3. Are the learning outcomes assessable?
4. Does the semester structure move from concept to evidence to failure diagnosis to design action?
5. Are weekly outputs visible and cumulative?
6. Is the final project defensible as architectural design/research synthesis without becoming a whole-building redesign?
7. Are thermodynamics and building-physics basics explicit enough to justify the technical course identity?
8. Does A4 show climate-stress awareness without becoming a full climate-futures redesign?
9. Does the tool stack look manageable and supported?
10. Is there a fallback route if EnergyPlus, Rhino, sensors, CFD, or other tools fail?
11. Does the course include mid-semester feedback and adjustment?
12. Does the course produce accreditation-relevant evidence without overclaiming accreditation coverage?

## Risk Flags

- Course title too narrow or too technical.
- No visible architectural outputs.
- Too much dependence on one software installation.
- Assessments too large, insufficiently scaffolded, or framed as a full redesign.
- Comfort models treated as truth rather than interpretive frameworks.
- Thermal evidence not translated into design action.
- No clear uncertainty/reproducibility standard.
- Equations appear as isolated technical content rather than assessable design reasoning.
- Optional CFD or fire-engineering extensions become implied requirements.
- Roll-forward validation becomes too broad or computationally unfair.

## Preferred Program-Director Reading

This course is a rigorous MArch elective that teaches students to interpret thermal environments as spatial, temporal, human-centered, and uncertain design conditions. It supports technical knowledge, environmental responsibility, health/welfare reasoning, research skills, and design synthesis through visible architectural outputs and one bounded thermal design action package.
