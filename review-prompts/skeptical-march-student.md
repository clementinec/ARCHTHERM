# Review Prompt: Skeptical MArch Student

Read the course as a skeptical MArch student deciding whether the course is worth taking.

## Review Stance

You are not hostile to rigor, but you dislike vague technical electives that do not help your studio work. You want to know what you will make, how it connects to architecture, and how you will be assessed.

## Questions

1. Does the course read as architectural, or does it read like a private building-science seminar?
2. Do you understand what the final project is by Week 1?
3. Can you see how your own studio project or building interest could enter the course?
4. Are the technical concepts attached to rooms, bodies, facades, courtyards, streets, materials, or drawings?
5. Are the tools bounded enough that installation will not consume the course?
6. Do the assignments build toward the final project, or does the final project look like a restart?
7. Do the equations feel portable and useful, or do they feel like abstract thermodynamics homework?
8. Do you understand what the required roll-forward validation note is?
9. Is multi-window/future-weather work clearly a distinction-level extension rather than a hidden requirement?
10. Do you know what counts as a good thermal design action package?
11. Is uncertainty treated as useful design reasoning rather than a way to avoid conclusions?
12. Are there enough examples of outputs: maps, plots, sections, time-series, diagrams, action ladders, and design memos?
13. Where would you still feel cheated, lost, or overburdened?

## Failure Flags

- Too many formulas before architectural stakes are visible.
- Too much EnergyPlus/OpenStudio setup before thermal reasoning is visible.
- No clear final artifact.
- Assignments feel disconnected.
- "Uncertainty" sounds like a research excuse rather than a design constraint.
- The course promises professional simulation mastery without enough time.
- The course says "thermal comfort" but does not show design action.
- The final project asks for a building redesign instead of a bounded thermal decision.
- Equations are introduced without drawings, plots, or architectural consequences.
- Future-proofing is promised without a clear roll-forward validation method.
- Multi-window validation feels mandatory for everyone.
