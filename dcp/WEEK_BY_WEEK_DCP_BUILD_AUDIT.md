## Session 2 Diagnostic Round Needs

Session 2 is conceptually strong, but each week needs a small prompt card.

Each prompt card should include:

- what image the student should bring;
- what classmates should guess;
- what mechanism should be named;
- what evidence would be needed to verify the guess;
- what design action might follow;
- how much hinting is allowed that week.

Hint taper:

- Weeks 1-3: generous hints and structured categories.
- Weeks 4-8: fewer hints, mechanism names provided only when needed.
- Weeks 9-11: minimal hints; students should name mechanism, failure mode, and action logic.

## Rubric Needs

Each assignment should have a short rubric aligned to:

- physical correctness;
- spatial/temporal legibility;
- evidence quality;
- uncertainty ownership;
- design consequence;
- reproducibility.

A4 also needs:

- action-mechanism fit;
- roll-forward validation quality;
- final oral defense clarity.

## Risk Notes

- The course is intellectually heavy. It becomes manageable only if weekly outputs are small, visual, and cumulative.
- Do not let optional CFD, advanced EnergyPlus, or radiant hardware become required workload.
- Do not let the final project become a climate-redesign thesis. A4 is one design action plus one changed stress assumption.
- The DCP should not mention unfinished starter kits. Internal pages can state WIP, but the official DCP should present prepared outputs and bounded optional tools.
- The pedagogical promise is not "thermal engineering for architects." It is architecturally legible thermal reasoning under uncertainty.
