# Roll-Forward Validation

Roll-forward validation is the A4 mechanism that connects thermal evidence to future-proofing without turning the final project into a whole climate-change thesis.

## Core Question

> Does the proposed design action still make sense when the climate stress, weather window, or evaluation assumption changes?

## Required Version

Every final project includes one roll-forward validation note.

Minimum:

- keep the design action fixed
- change one stress assumption
- report whether the action is robust, weakened, censored, or unsupported
- state one future-proofing implication
- own one limitation honestly

Possible changed assumptions:

- hotter week
- heatwave instead of typical week
- future or climate-adjusted weather file
- different stress-window length
- different occupancy or use schedule
- changed glazing/window assumption
- indoor/outdoor boundary shift
- altered ventilation or fan availability

## Distinction-Level Version

Students with stronger tool access may perform a multi-window or multi-file validation.

Examples:

- 3-day versus 7-day versus 14-day heat period
- typical week versus hot week versus heatwave week
- current EPW versus future/climate-adjusted EPW
- baseline window versus low-SHGC window while keeping all other assumptions fixed
- dry-hot versus humid-hot periods

This is similar to cross-validation or segment-length sensitivity. The student is not just optimizing for one cherry-picked period; they are checking whether the design conclusion survives a different slice of climate stress.

## What Counts As Good Work

Good roll-forward validation:

- changes only one or two assumptions at a time
- keeps the design action stable enough to compare
- reports a clear outcome label
- explains why the conclusion changed or survived
- translates that result into a design implication

Poor roll-forward validation:

- runs many scenarios with no interpretation
- changes the design and weather at the same time
- claims future-proofing from one typical weather file
- hides censored or unresolved outcomes
- treats more computation as automatically better design reasoning

## Outcome Labels

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Robust | action still meets or plausibly supports the target under changed stress |
| Weakened | action helps, but with reduced margin or more residual burden |
| Censored | action does not reach the target within tested assumptions |
| Unsupported | evidence is insufficient to claim a roll-forward conclusion |
